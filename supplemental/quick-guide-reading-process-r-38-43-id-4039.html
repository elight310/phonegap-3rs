Quick Guide to the Three-Step Reading Process

Following a reading processwill help you become abetter reader of all types of materials.Reading is a personal process through which youmake meaning using your own experiences, knowledge, and abilities. There are, however, certain common features found in the process all readers follow. The reading process falls into threestages: Before Reading, During Reading, and After Reading.

BEFORE READING

The first part of the reading process takes place before you ever begin to read.

Set a Purpose

Understand your purpose, so you can be a better reader. Your teachers often determine your reading purpose. Tohelp you clarify your purpose and evaluate information, you mighttry to answer the following questions:Who?What? When? Where? Why?How?

Preview

Preview a reading assignment with a quick skim-through so you will know what to expect. A preview of a reading gives you important information about vocabulary, organization, and content. A preview may also tell you that a textwill not answer your questions and that you should not spendvaluable time reading it.

Plan

Strategies lead to success in reading. For some reading assignments, note taking may be a good strategy. For others, graphic organizers,paraphrasing, visualizing and thinking aloud, outlining, skimming, or other strategies may be useful.

DURING READING

The middle part of the readingprocess is what happens whenyou are actually reading.

Read with a Purpose

  • Read actively and interact with the text. Asking yourself questions, having a discussion, taking notes, and using graphic organizers all make you a more active reader.
  • Keep a close eye on your progress as you read. If what you are reading makes no sense, stop and turn around.
  • Revisit your original purpose. You might have to read thesame passage up to three times, each time with a different purpose.
  • Check your understanding as you read. Good readers make predictions and clarify what they’ve learned, then check to seewhether their predictions and understanding are correct.

Connect

  • Try to tie what you read to your life or to what you already know. Ask yourself some questions like these to help you connect:

How do I feel after reading this? Why?

Do I agree with the author’s point of view?

Have I read anything else by this author?

How does this affect me?

AFTER READING

This is the stage of the reading process that happens after you have finished reading the last sentence.

Pause and Reflect

  • Think about what you have read. Have you understood what you just read? Have you achieved your stated purpose?
  • Fill in the gaps. Identify anything that puzzles you and determine whether you can find the information on your own or if you need to ask your teacher for help.

Reread

Rereading helps you experience a selection again in a new way. Younow know a lot more, so when you start to reread you have a betterbase of knowledge to start from.

  • Use another reading strategy when you reread. To find the information you need, you might have to outline or do close reading the second time through a selection.

Remember

The key to remembering is to make the information your own. Some techniques for remembering reading include:

  • creating a summary
  • writing a review
  • capturing the information in a graphic organizer
  • starting a conversation
  • inventing a memory aid (such as a rhyme)

You just need to find a strategy or technique that works well for you.
