Using Graphic Organizers for Studying

Graphic organizers are visual pictures of ideas. You create them using symbols, images and text to help you organize, see, and interpret important ideas in a reading. There are also text-based organizers, such as note-taking templates and outlines. Text-based organizers can be a good choice for gathering, categorizing and arranging certain types of information.

Using organizers helps you be a more active reader. Use an organizer as you read to identify and connect different ideas, make comparisons, and examine and analyze relationships. An organizer can help you keep track of details, such as point of view, characters, setting, plot, theme, and style.

Organizers are effective study tools. Creating an organizer helps you clarify your thoughts and ideas, understand new concepts more thoroughly, and improve your recall of the material. The organizers you create are ideal for study and review because they represent your interpretations in a form that is personally relevant to you. 

Basic Organizers

Basic organizers are flexible, multifunctional tools. You might develop a concept map or a mind map to organize what you know about a topic and understand the relationships and connections among ideas. A Venn diagram may be used to compare and contrast any two things. A web provides a flexible framework for organizing and prioritizing information, and  can be used for any type of note taking, and an outline may be a good choice to for textbook notes. Sequencing tools, such a timeline or a storyboard, help you keep track of dates and events. A flowchart can document the steps of a procedure, for example, in science.

In addition to the basic set of organizers, there is a collection of organizers and templates designed to help with specific reading and studying tasks.
