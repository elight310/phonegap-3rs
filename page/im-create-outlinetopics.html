Topics and subtopics – add, select, move, and change relationships
Outlines are organized lists of information consisting of main points and subtopics that support the main points. If you use an outline to develop an essay or research paper you will also want to use Inspiration® Maps™ notes to add research notes or start to expand upon your topics or subtopics. Often outlines start as lists, but as you review your list you start to rearrange and group information under key points, creating your subtopics. Doing this allows you to identify where you have too much information for your paper, or too little. This guides what additional refinement of the content you need to do before you start writing. Inspiration Maps electronic outliner makes it easy to add, organize and edit your work.
 
In Inspiration Maps, we call the main points topics and supporting points subtopics, following formal outlining terminology. Generally topics and subtopics are written as key words or phrases. You might, at some point, be asked to create and turn in a full sentence outline, but when using outlining for thinking and organizing it is best to keep to key words or phrases.
 
Inspiration Maps has a set of easy-to-use outlining tools to help you create, organize and structure your outline quickly.  These tools are explained below.
 

 

Add a topic or subtopic
Inspiration Maps provides two ways to add a topic or subtopic to your outline. Use any of the following:

  1. Double tap below the last topic in your outline to add a new topic. The topic will be added at the end of your outline.
  2. With a topic selected, tap  on the . This will add the topic below the selected topic. If the selected topic has subtopics that are showing, the topic will be added as a subtopic. If the topic does not have subtopics, or they are hidden, the new topic will be added at the same level as the topic.

Add text to a topic or subtopic

  1. Double tap the topic. This will bring up the keyboard, ready for you to start typing. For more information on editing text, see Help topic – Create Outlines: Text – add, edit, style and change prefix labels.

Add or launch a hyperlink
Inspiration Maps recognizes hyperlinks when you type the standard worldwide web (www.) prefix and text into a topic or subtopic.  All the text added to the topic or subtopic is then part of the hyperlink.  Inspiration Maps does not support launching of hyperlinks that are in a topic or subtopic's Note.
 
To add a hyperlink to a topic or subtopic:

  1. If you are not already in text editing, double tap the topic to bring up the keyboard.
  2. Type in the hyperlink's text starting with www. Once you finish adding text to the topic, Inspiration Maps will change the text font to show that it recognized it as a hyperlink.

To launch a topic or subtopic's hyperlink:

  1. With a topic selected that contains a hyperlink, tap  on the . This will launch the hyperlink. 

Select a topic or subtopic
To work with a topic or subtopic, you need to select it first.

  1. Tap to select it. To deselect it, select a different topic or tap in an open area at the bottom of your outline. 

Delete a topic or subtopic
When you delete a topic, if it has subtopics or a note connected to it the subtopics and note will also be deleted.
To delete a topic or subtopic:

  1. Select the topic or subtopic to be deleted.
  2. Tap  on the toolbar to delete the item. If you delete a topic or subtopic by mistake, tap  on the toolbar right away to restore the deleted item.

Move and reorder topics
Dragging topics to a new location easily moves them. If the topic has subtopics, the subtopics will move with it.

  1. Tap to select the topic to move.
  2. Touch  to the right of the topic and drag your finger to move the topic up or down in the outline hierarchy. Release the drag to complete moving the topic.

Change topic and subtopic relationships
When you are working on an outline, topics are rearranged and priorities change. You may want a topic that started out as a main topic to be subordinate to the topic above it.  There may also be subtopics that you want to move out a level so that they are no longer subordinate topics.
To change a topic into a subtopic:

  1. Tap the topic you want to move and turn into a subtopic.
  2. Tap  on the  to move the topic under the topic above it, making it a subtopic.

 
To make a subtopic into a main topic:

  1. Tap the subtopic you want to move out from under a topic.
  2. Tap  on the  to change the subtopic into a topic.

Cut, copy, and paste a topic and its subtopics
You can easily cut, copy, and paste a topic within a outline. When you cut or copy a topic its subtopics will also be cut or copied.
To do this:

  1. Tap and hold your finger on the topic that you want to cut or copy.
  2. Release your finger to bring up the  bar.
  3. Choose one of the options:
  • Cut will place the topic and its subtopics in the iPad clipboard and remove it from the outline.
  • Copy will place a copy of the topic and its subtopics in the iPad clipboard and leave the original in your outline.
  • Paste will insert what is in the iPad clipboard below the selected topic.

Add a note to a topic or subtopic
Topics are great for recording ideas and information in a couple of words or short phrases, but when you want to develop an outline topic into a paragraph or add research information, use the topic’s note. Notes will appear under their topic in a smaller font size.
To add a note:

  1. Tap the topic or subtopic to select it. Tap  on the bar to open the topic’s note. This will bring up the keyboard, ready for you to start typing. For more information on editing text, see Help topic – Create Outlines: Text – add, edit, style and change prefix labels.  If the topic already has a note this option will not be available.

Edit an existing note
If a topic already has a note, double tap the topic in the note area to edit it. This will bring up the keyboard, ready for you to start editing.
