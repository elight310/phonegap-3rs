Answering Multiple-Choice Questions

For many multiple-choice questions, you must decide if asentence or passage contains some type of error. You may be asked toidentify the error (if, in fact, one exists) or to correct it.

When you answer multiple-choice questions, keep thesepoints in mind:

  • Read the question carefully. Youwill be asked to analyze a brief passage, to determine if it contains an error, and, possibly, to correct the error.
  • Watch for negatives. When directions containwords such as not, none, never, and except, makesure you understand what is being asked.
  • Read each answer carefully. Watch for answers such as “Both A and B,” “All of the above,” or “None of the above.”
  • Eliminate some answers. Cross out choices you know are not correct before you make a choice.

Look for different clues depending on the focus of the test:

Testing use of conventions
Testing sentence revision skills
Testing paragraph revisions skills
Testing editing skills

 

TESTING USE OF CONVENTIONS

Multiple-choice questions may deal with the rules of language: punctuation, capitalization, spelling, grammar, and usage.  Here are the types of questions you may be asked.

Example 1

Brazil is a vast country, it is almost as big as the United States.

The best choice for the highlighted section is

  1. NO CHANGE
  2. country: it
  3. country; it
  4. country it

This item focuses on punctuation. The answer is C.

 

Example 2

A hush past over the crowd as Kurt, our best gymnast, fell from thehigh bar. Coach Bryant and I ran over and tried to determine whetherhe was injured, but we wouldn’t know if his ankle was all right untilthe next day when X-rays were taken.

Which one of the highlighted words is not the right word?

  1. past
  2. whether
  3. know
  4. all right

This item focuses on words that sound the same and areoften mixed up in writing—passed/past, weather/whether,know/no, and alright/all right. The answer is A.

 

Example 3

“It’s over they’re,” the salesperson said, pointing out the store’s sale item.

The best choice for the highlighted section is

  1. “It’s over their,”
  2. “Its over there,”
  3. “Its over they’re,”
  4. “It’s over there,”

This item also focuses on words that sound the same andare often mixed up in writing—it’s/its and their/there/they’re. The answer is D.

 

Example 4

According to our science teacher, the Mesozoic Era came after the Permia Period.

Which one of the highlighted words has a capitalization error?

  1. science
  2. Mesozoic Era
  3. Permian Period
  4. NONE

This item focuses on capitalization. The answer is D.

 

Example 5

According to the most reliable calculations, the Leaning Tower of Pisahave existed for at least 650 years.

Which of the following is the correct change for the highlighted words?

  1. NO CHANGE
  2. will have existed
  3. exists
  4. has existed

This item focuses on verb tenses and subject-verbagreement. The answer is D.

 

Example 6

He and the other players sat in silence; they were stunned by thier sudden defeat.

Which of the highlighted items should be corrected?

  1. He
  2. silence;
  3. thier
  4. Both B and D

This item focuses on spelling. The answer is C.

TESTING SENTENCE REVISION SKILLS

Other multiple-choice questions may test your ability to revise sentences. These questions cover such skills as combining sentences, fixing ambiguouswording, creating parallel structure, correcting dangling modifiers, and eliminating sentence errors. (See the “Editing and ProofreadingMarks” in Resources.)

Read the following sentences and choose the best revision.If the original is the best choice, the answer is D.

Example 1

The weather which consisted of seven straight days of rain. It ruinedour vacation.

  1. The weather, which consisted of seven straight days ofrain, ruined our vacation.
  2. The weather consisted of seven straight days of rain. Andit ruined our vacation.
  3. The weather that consisted of seven straight days of rain.It ruined our vacation.
  4. NO CHANGE

This item focuses on sentence fragments and sentencecombining. The answer is A.

 

Example 2

Whipping the willow’s branches back and forth, we huddled at thescreen door to watch the wind.

  1. To watch the wind, we huddled at the screen door,whipping the willow’s branches back and forth.
  2. We huddled at the screen door to watch the windwhipping the willow’s branches back and forth.
  3. Watching the wind whipping the willow’s branches backand forth at the screen door, we huddled.
  4. NO CHANGE

This item focuses on misplaced modifiers.The answer is B.

 

Example 3

Natasha went to the restaurant and ate a sub sandwich and drank adiet cola and then she went back to work.

  1. Natasha went to the restaurant and ate a sub sandwichand drank a diet cola. Then she went back to work.
  2. Natasha went to the restaurant, and ate a sub sandwich,and drank a diet cola. Then she went back to work.
  3. Natasha went to the restaurant, where she had a subsandwich and a diet cola before going back to work.
  4. NO CHANGE

This item focuses on rambling or awkward sentences.The answer is C.

 

Example 4

I like sushi better than my brother.

  1. I like sushi more better than my brother.
  2. I like sushi better than my brother does.
  3. I like sushi even better than my brother.
  4. NO CHANGE

This item focuses on incomplete comparisons.The answer is B.

 

Example 5

No one saw Saul’s boat sink after the lightning hit it, which Saulthought was just one more example of his bad luck.

  1. Saul thought it was just one more example of his bad luckthat no one saw his boat sink after the lightning hit it.
  2. No one saw Saul’s boat sink after the lightning hit it. Saulthought it was just one more example of his bad luck.
  3. After the lightning hit Saul’s boat, no one saw it sink.
  4. Saul thought it was just one more example of his bad luck.
  5. NO CHANGE

This item focuses on ambiguous wording. The answer is A.

 

Example 6

The car raced down the highway. The highway had ice on it. Thecar leaned into a sharp curve. It spun out of control and slid over theembankment.

  1. The car raced down the highway, and the highway hadice on it. The car leaned into a sharp curve and spun outof control and slid over the embankment.
  2. The car raced down the icy highway. The car leaned intoa sharp curve and spun out of control. It slid over theembankment.
  3. The car raced down the icy highway, leaned into a sharpcurve, spun out of control, and slid over the embankment.
  4. NO CHANGE

This item focuses on sentence combining. The answer is C.

 

Example 7

The basketball player, with one black sock and one yellow one, scoredthe most points.

  1. The basketball player, with one black sock and one yellowone scored the most points.
  2. The basketball player with one black sock and one yellowone, scored the most points.
  3. The basketball player with one black sock and one yellowone scored the most points.
  4. NO CHANGE

This item focuses on restrictive/nonrestrictive phrases andclauses. The answer is C.

TESTING PARAGRAPH REVISION SKILLS

Some questions are based on an extended reading sample. These questions test your paragraph-revising skills. It is important to read the example essay carefully and refer to it as you try to answer the questions below.

Question 1

Which of the following would be better than sentence 1?

  1. On a sleepy Saturday morning, you suddenly wake upbecause you feel your dog’s cold, wet nose on your hand—a signal that it’s time to let him outside.
  2. I’d like to talk about a dog’s nose.
  3. A dog’s nose is an extraordinarily perceptive device.
  4. NO CHANGE

This item focuses on openings or introductions.The answer is A.

 

Question 2

Which of the following sentences should be deleted?

  1. Sentence 8
  2. Sentence 13
  3. Sentence 23
  4. NONE

This item focuses on paragraph unity. The answer is C.

 

Question 3

Which of the following would be the best addition to the beginning ofsentence 12?

  1. On the other hand,
  2. First of all,
  3. Likewise,
  4. In other words,

This item focuses on transitions. The answer is B.

 

Question 4

Which sentence best paraphrases the focus of the essay?

  1. Medical science is learning more and more about howdogs can help humans.
  2. Humans cannot get along without a dog’s help.
  3. Dogs should be given greater respect.
  4. A dog’s keen sense of smell has wide-ranging benefits tohumans.

This item focuses on the main idea or thesis.The answer is D.

 

Question 5

If you were to add a sentence to the end of the essay, which of thefollowing would best reflect the way the writer feels about the topic?

  1. Dogs can be pretty goofy, but sometimes they’re a big help.
  2. The preponderance of evidence says that dogs arebeneficial.
  3. All I can say about dogs is, “Cool!”
  4. The animals that have long been considered faithfulcompanions may also be able to help people live betterlives.

This item focuses on tone. The answer is D.

TESTING EDITING SKILLS

Some writing tests include questions that assess your editing skills.These questions require you to find a usage or grammatical error in asentence. In the following sentences, choose the part of the sentence that contains an error. If there is no error, choose D.

Example 1

Inez heard the phone ringing, answered it, and has taken the message.

  1. heard the phone ringing
  2. answered it
  3. has taken the message
  4. NO ERROR

This item focuses on parallel structure. The answer is C.

 

Example 2

We recommend that new rules be considered at this point in time.

  1. recommend
  2. be considered
  3. at this point in time
  4. NO ERROR

This item focuses on ineffective expressions, or “deadwood.”The answer is C.

 

Example 3

She had a habit of speaking too softly when she was saying something.

  1. had
  2. too
  3. when she was saying something
  4. NO ERROR

This item focuses on wordiness, or redundancy.The answer is C.

Example 4

Sam, an apprentice, he was afraid he would lose his job, but the boss thought Sam was doing good work.

  1. he
  2. his
  3. thought
  4. NO ERROR

This item focuses on double subjects. The answer is A.

 

Example 5

As she swung her bat at the ball, it whistled through the air.

  1. swung
  2. it
  3. through
  4. NO ERROR

This item focuses on indefinite reference. The answer is B.

 

Example 6

One of my friend’s favorite books are To Kill a Mockingbird.

  1. friend’s
  2. are
  3. a
  4. NO ERROR

This item focuses on subject-verb agreement.The answer is B.
